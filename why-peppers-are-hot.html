Why peppers are hot

Here's why peppers are hot

We all sometimes like to be "well-off", though we are often surprised at how hot a pepper can be. When this happens to us, we cannot help but wonder why the pepper is hot and what affects its hotter.
 Why is pepper hot?
 What makes peppers mad?
Capsaicin is a natural alkaloid made from hot peppers. This alkaloid is to blame for the pepper's characteristic hot taste. Different types of peppers and pepperoni contain different amounts of capsaicin.
Capsaicin or capsacin is actually a very complex material. It does not dissolve in water, but it dissolves readily in alcohol and oils. When applied to the skin, it causes burning sensation and hyperemia (increased circulation in the treated area), which helps to relieve rheumatic pain.

 What Affects Pepper Pepper?
Peppers can be very hot, weak, or may simply not contain hot. It depends on the greater or lesser presence of capsaicin in the fetus. Small bell peppers are hotter than large ones, while mature ones are more angry than green ones. And those from the southern and warmer regions are definitely hotter than those from the northern and colder ones.
 Only for the most durable - the hottest peppers sauces in the world!
 In one study in Japan, it was found that high night and day temperatures can also influence the increase in capsaicin content, which is why the hot pepper in hot areas is higher. Also, hot peppers from winter production in the protected area are sweeter than summer ones.
